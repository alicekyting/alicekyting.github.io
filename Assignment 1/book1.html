Shadow and Bone

"Shadow and Bone" is a fantasy novel by Leigh Bardugo that was first published in 2012.
The story is set in a world called Ravka, where the kingdom is divided by a massive and dangerous swath of darkness called the Fold.
The novel follows the journey of Alina Starkov, a young orphan who discovers that she possesses a rare and powerful ability that could be the key to saving her country from the darkness.
The story is compelling and the world-building is impressive, with a unique magic system and complex political dynamics.

The characters are well-drawn and engaging, particularly Alina, who is a relatable and sympathetic protagonist.
For me, one of the standout aspects of "Shadow and Bone" is the romance between Alina and the enigmatic and charming General Kirigan.
Their relationship is fraught with tension and uncertainty, as Alina struggles to reconcile her growing feelings for him with her doubts about his true intentions.

Overall, "Shadow and Bone" is an engaging and enjoyable read that will appeal to fans of epic fantasy and romantic drama.
The novel sets up a compelling world and introduces a cast of memorable characters, leaving readers eager to dive deeper into the series.
